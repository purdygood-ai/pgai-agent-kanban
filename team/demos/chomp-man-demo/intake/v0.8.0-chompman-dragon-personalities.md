## Overview
Give the four dragons distinct movement AI so the maze has real chase dynamics
instead of uniform behavior. Each dragon gets a named identity and a movement
personality, mirroring the classic four-ghost archetypes. The dragon SPRITES
(winged silhouettes) shipped in v0.6.0 and the warp tunnels shipped in v0.7.0 —
this RC is about how each dragon MOVES and DECIDES, not how it looks.

This is a behavioral-variety feature: four clearly-distinguishable movement
patterns that the player can observe and learn. It exercises the release workflow
on real game-logic work and produces good demo footage (four dragons visibly
behaving differently).

## The four dragons

| # | Identity   | Movement personality |
|---|------------|----------------------|
| 1 | `rover`    | Random roaming — picks a legal direction at each decision point with no target; wanders the maze. |
| 2 | `drifter`  | Random roaming — same random-wander behavior as `rover` (a second independent random roamer, so two dragons roam unpredictably). |
| 3 | `tracker`  | Direction-follower — biases its movement toward Chomp Man's current direction/position, but ONLY while Chomp Man has NOT eaten a power pellet. Loosely pursues by heading the way Chomp Man is going. |
| 4 | `hunter`   | Direct chaser — actively pursues Chomp Man's current tile, choosing at each decision point the legal direction that reduces distance to him (greedy chase). |

(Names are suggestions — keep the FOUR functional behaviors exactly as described;
the identity labels can be renamed to fit the existing code's naming conventions.
The functional intent is: two random roamers, one soft-follower gated on the
power-pellet state, one hard chaser.)

## Behavior detail (the functional contract)
1. **`rover` (random roamer):** At each maze decision point (intersection or when
   blocked), choose uniformly at random among the legal non-reversing directions
   (avoid immediate 180° reversals unless it's the only legal move). No awareness
   of Chomp Man. Independent RNG so it does not lock-step with `drifter`.

2. **`drifter` (random roamer):** Identical behavior to `rover` — a second
   independent random roamer. Independent random stream so the two roamers diverge
   over time (they should NOT mirror each other).

3. **`tracker` (direction-follower, power-pellet-gated):**
   - While Chomp Man has NOT eaten a power pellet (normal state): bias movement
     toward Chomp Man — at each decision point prefer the legal direction that
     heads in Chomp Man's current direction / toward his position. It is a SOFT
     follower (heads "the way he's going" / toward him), not necessarily a perfect
     shortest-path chaser.
   - While a power pellet IS active (Chomp Man has eaten one and the effect is
     running): `tracker` STOPS following — it should revert to fleeing or random
     roaming (consistent with however the game already handles power-pellet/
     frightened state for dragons). The defining trait is that its following
     behavior is GATED on the no-power-pellet condition.

4. **`hunter` (direct chaser):** At each decision point, choose the legal
   non-reversing direction that minimizes distance (Manhattan or maze-aware) to
   Chomp Man's current tile — a greedy chaser that actively closes in. Like all
   dragons, it must respect the existing power-pellet/frightened behavior (flee or
   become vulnerable) when a power pellet is active.

## Constraints / preserve existing behavior
- **Build on the existing dragon system** (v0.6.0 sprites, v0.7.0 warp tunnels).
  Do NOT regress the winged-silhouette sprites or the left↔right warp-tunnel
  wrap. Dragons must still use the warp tunnels like Chomp Man does (if they did
  before) or at least not break board traversal.
- **Respect the existing power-pellet / frightened state.** All four dragons must
  honor whatever the game already does when a power pellet is eaten (e.g. become
  edible / flee). `tracker`'s follow-gating and `hunter`'s chase must both yield to
  the frightened state. Do not introduce a dragon that ignores power pellets.
- **The board must remain clearable / the game winnable.** Four dragons with a
  hard chaser must not make the game impossible — the chaser should be beatable
  with normal play (this was the integrity concern for warp tunnels too). If
  difficulty becomes unfair, tune the chaser's decision cadence, not its identity.
- **No new external dependencies.** Pure pygame + existing game modules.
- **Determinism for the roamers:** seed or stream the RNG so `rover` and `drifter`
  are independent (they must not produce identical paths).
- **All four dragons spawn and are active** in normal play (not behind a flag).

## Acceptance Criteria
1. The game runs and is playable with four dragons active in normal play.
2. Two dragons (`rover`, `drifter`) move by random roaming and visibly diverge
   from each other over time (independent random streams — not identical paths).
3. One dragon (`tracker`) demonstrably heads toward / follows Chomp Man's
   direction while NO power pellet is active, and STOPS following (reverts to
   flee/roam) while a power pellet IS active.
4. One dragon (`hunter`) demonstrably closes distance on Chomp Man (greedy chase)
   during normal play.
5. All four dragons honor the existing power-pellet/frightened behavior (none
   ignores power pellets).
6. v0.6.0 sprites and v0.7.0 warp tunnels are NOT regressed (winged silhouettes
   intact; left↔right wrap still works; board still traversable).
7. The board remains clearable — the game is winnable with normal play.
8. Code-level: a clear per-dragon behavior assignment exists (grep-able — e.g. the
   four strategies are named/assigned distinctly), so TESTER can confirm four
   distinct behaviors are wired, not one behavior copy-pasted.
9. No new external dependencies; pure pygame + existing modules.
