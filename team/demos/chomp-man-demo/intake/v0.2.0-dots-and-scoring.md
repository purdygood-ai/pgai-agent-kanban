## Overview
Give the maze something to eat and a reason to move. This release scatters collectible
pellets through the maze corridors, lets Chomp Man eat them by moving over them, tracks a
score, and shows the score on screen. When all pellets are eaten, the player has cleared the
board (win condition).

## Goals
1. Pellets placed in the open corridors of the maze (every traversable cell, or a sensible
   subset — your design, but the maze should be densely dotted like the genre).
2. Chomp Man eats a pellet by entering its cell; the pellet disappears.
3. A score that increases per pellet eaten.
4. The score displayed on screen (HUD), updating live.
5. A win/clear condition when all pellets are eaten (a simple "BOARD CLEAR" state/message).

## Design / Theming
- Call the collectibles something on-theme if you like (e.g. "embers" or "crumbs") — but
  "pellets"/"dots" is fine. Keep them visually distinct and readable.
- Scoring values are your choice but be consistent and document them in code comments
  (suggested: 10 points per standard pellet, to leave room for higher-value items later).
- The HUD (score display) should be clean and not obscure the maze.

## Deliverables
- Updated game code:
  - Pellets placed into the maze grid (reuse the v0.1.0 maze structure).
  - Eat-on-enter logic (Chomp Man entering a pellet cell removes it and adds score).
  - A score counter, incremented on eat, rendered in a HUD.
  - A board-clear condition + on-screen indication when all pellets are gone.

## Acceptance criteria
1. `python3 -m py_compile` passes for all changed/added .py files.
2. Pellets render throughout the maze corridors (manual visual check) and do not appear inside
   walls (verify placement uses the open cells of the maze grid).
3. Moving Chomp Man over a pellet removes that pellet and increases the score (verify the
   eat-and-score logic in code; manual confirm visually).
4. The score is displayed on screen and updates as pellets are eaten.
5. Eating all pellets triggers a board-clear state/message (verify the win condition exists
   and fires when the pellet count reaches zero).
6. Scoring values are defined as named constants (not magic numbers scattered in the logic),
   so later items (power-ups, fruit) can add their own values cleanly.

## Suggested Decomposition
- CODER-1: pellet placement into the maze grid + rendering.
- CODER-2: eat-on-enter detection + score counter + HUD rendering.
- CODER-3: board-clear (win) condition + message.

## Notes for CODER
Reuse the v0.1.0 maze grid for pellet placement — do not introduce a parallel structure.
Define scoring as named constants so v0.4.0 (power-ups) and v0.5.0 (fruit) can extend scoring
without touching this logic. Keep the eat check a simple cell-occupancy test against Chomp
Man's grid position. The board-clear condition is just "no pellets remain" — keep it simple;
fancier game-over/restart flow can come later.

## Notes for TESTER

logic removes the pellet and increments score; a win condition checks for zero remaining
pellets; scoring uses named constants (grep for the constant names, confirm no bare magic
