## Overview

Bring the fox back into the story. This tests reintroduction/reconstruction: can
the workflow re-add a previously-removed element coherently, re-establishing the
scene and any callbacks, so the story reads as a unified whole again? It also
tests a subtle judgment: does "bring back the fox" mean restore the original
fox scene, or freshly reintegrate it into the (now slightly different, post-
blueberry-scene) story?

## The change

Reintroduce the fox-and-stream scene: the bears, on their daytime walk, meet the
friendly fox at the clear stream, watch the silver fish together, and part with
the fox's gentle farewell. The fox should once again be woven into the story
coherently — placed correctly relative to the other scenes (the walk, the
blueberry scene if present, the cabin bedtime arc).

## What "bring back" should mean (judgment under test)

The fox scene existed in v0.0.2/v0.0.3 and was removed in v0.0.4. "Bring it back"
can be satisfied by restoring that scene — but it must be reintegrated into the
CURRENT story state (v0.0.4), which may differ from v0.0.2 (e.g. if a blueberry
scene exists, the fox scene and blueberry scene must coexist in correct
chronological order: stream/fox first, then blueberries on the walk home, then
the cabin). The workflow should not blindly paste the old scene if doing so
creates a contradiction with the current text; it should reintegrate.

## Constraints

- The reintroduced fox scene should match the established voices and tone
  (consistent with how it read in v0.0.2).
- It must be placed in the correct narrative/chronological position relative to
  whatever else is in v0.0.4 (the walk, any interior scene, the cabin arc).
- Any thematic callbacks that made the fox scene resonate (Little Bear's shyness-
  then-wonder echoing the firefly wonder) may be re-established.
- Preserve the rest of the v0.0.4 story.
- Published to projects/pgai-three-bears/artifacts/v0.0.5-*.md (prior versions retained).

## Acceptance / what the operator will judge (prose — human read + diff)

- The fox/stream scene is present again and reads coherently.
- It is correctly placed relative to the other scenes (no chronological
  contradiction — e.g. fox-at-stream before walk-home blueberries before cabin).
- The story reads as a unified whole, not a paste of an old scene into a changed
  story.
- The operator may diff against v0.0.2/v0.0.3 to see whether the fox came back
  faithfully and whether reintegration (vs. blind restore) was handled.
