## Overview

Remove the fox-and-stream scene from the story — and remove it CLEANLY, so that
the story reads as if the fox had never been in it. This tests dangling-reference
cleanup: the fox scene seeded callbacks and thematic echoes elsewhere; a
comprehending removal finds and resolves them, a lazy removal deletes the scene
but leaves orphaned references.

## The change

Remove the daytime fox-and-stream scene (the bears walking to the stream,
meeting the fox, watching the silver fish, the fox's farewell). The story should
return to being centered on the cabin bedtime arc (the missing star / firefly).

## Dangling-reference cleanup (the hard part — this is what's being tested)

The fox scene seeded references that appear or resonate elsewhere. After removing
the scene, the document must not contain ORPHANED references to it. In
particular, check and resolve:

- Any later line that refers back to the fox, the stream, the silver fish, or
  the walk to the water (e.g. callbacks like "as the fox had said" or echoes of
  "the stream will be here tomorrow").
- The transition that led OUT of the fox scene into the rest of the story must
  be re-knit so the story opens or flows correctly without it.
- If v0.0.3's blueberry/walk-home scene depends on the fox scene having happened
  (e.g. "on the way home" implies they had walked somewhere), reconcile that —
  either keep the walk-home framing coherent on its own or adjust so nothing
  references a walk that no longer has its starting scene.
- Any thematic setup that only made sense because of the fox (e.g. Little Bear's
  shyness-then-wonder being introduced at the stream and paid off at the firefly)
  should still read coherently with the fox scene gone.

## The oracle (how the operator will judge)

A PERFECT fox removal that keeps the v0.0.3 blueberry scene would be close to
"v0.0.1 plus the blueberry scene." A perfect removal that ALSO drops the
blueberry scene (if it was fox-dependent) would be very close to v0.0.1 itself.
The operator will DIFF v0.0.4 against v0.0.1 and v0.0.3 to judge: did the workflow
surgically remove only the fox material and re-knit cleanly, or did it leave
orphans / over-remove / damage unrelated text?

## Constraints

- Remove ONLY the fox/stream material and its dependent references. Do NOT remove
  or damage the cabin/bedtime scene (the heart of the story) or the v0.0.3
  blueberry scene unless the blueberry scene is genuinely fox-dependent (in which
  case reconcile, don't just delete).
- No dangling references to the fox, stream, or silver fish anywhere in the
  result.
- Voices and tone preserved.
- Published to projects/pgai-three-bears/artifacts/v0.0.4-*.md (prior versions retained).

## Acceptance / what the operator will judge (prose — human read + diff)

- The fox/stream scene is gone.
- NO orphaned references to the fox/stream/fish remain anywhere.
- The story reads coherently start to finish as if the fox were never there.

- Diff against v0.0.1 shows the removal landed cleanly (result ~= v0.0.1 + any
  retained interior scene).
