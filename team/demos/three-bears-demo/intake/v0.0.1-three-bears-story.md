## Overview
Write a short, gentle bedtime story for young children (ages 4-7) featuring a
family of three friendly bears. This is a self-contained illustrated-story-style
text (no illustrations required — just the prose). Warm, simple, soothing tone
suitable for reading aloud at bedtime.

## Goals
1. A complete, original short story (roughly 500-800 words) starring three
   bears: a big bear, a middle-sized bear, and a little bear.
2. Age-appropriate vocabulary and sentence length for ages 4-7 — short
   sentences, simple words, a gentle rhythm that reads well aloud.
3. A calm, cozy bedtime arc: a small everyday adventure that resolves warmly and
   ends with the bears settling down to sleep.
4. A light, repeating refrain or phrase the child can anticipate (bedtime stories
   benefit from a little repetition).

## Deliverables
A single prose story document with:
- A title.
- The full story text (500-800 words), structured in short paragraphs.
- A warm, sleepy ending.

## Content notes
- Keep it gentle and reassuring — nothing scary, no peril beyond a tiny, easily
  resolved mix-up.
- The three bears should each have a small distinct trait (e.g. the little bear
  is curious, the middle bear is helpful, the big bear is calm).
- A simple theme is fine: sharing, helping, getting ready for bed, finding
  something that was lost.
- End on a cozy, settling-down-to-sleep note.

## Style Notes
- Read-aloud friendly: short sentences, gentle cadence, a touch of repetition.
- Present a soft, warm world (a cabin in the woods, a starry night).
- This is throwaway test material for verifying the document workflow — content
  quality matters less than the workflow producing a versioned artifact.
