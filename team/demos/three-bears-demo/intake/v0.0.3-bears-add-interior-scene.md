## Overview

Add a new scene to the story — but one that, by its nature, CANNOT simply be
prepended to the front or appended to the end. It must be woven into the
INTERIOR of the existing narrative, at the correct structural location. This
tests whether the document workflow comprehends the story's structure (where a
scene belongs in the sequence), not just whether it can bolt text onto an end.

## The change (intake the way a real user would write it)

Currently the story has two movements: the daytime walk to the stream (where the
bears meet the fox), and then the evening bedtime scene at the cabin (the missing
star / firefly). Add a NEW middle scene that takes place DURING THE WALK HOME,
AFTER the bears leave the fox at the stream but BEFORE they reach the cabin:

On the way home, the bears pass a clearing where they find a patch of wild
blueberries. They stop to eat a few. Little Bear eats too many and gets a blue
nose and blue paws; Middle Bear gently cleans him up ("Everything in its place,
even small bears"); Big Bear notes, in his slow calm way, that the sweetest
berries always grow where the most sun falls. Keep it short (one to three
paragraphs), gentle, and matched to the established voices. The scene should
deepen the walk-home stretch and lead naturally into "By the time they reached
the little wooden cabin..."

## Why this placement matters (do not just prepend/append)

The blueberry scene happens chronologically between the fox farewell ("Safe
walking... the stream will be here tomorrow") and the arrival home ("By the time
they reached the little wooden cabin at the edge of the forest, the first stars
were just beginning to show"). It MUST be inserted at that interior seam, not at
the start or end of the document. A version that prepends or appends it has
failed to comprehend the structure.

## Constraints

- PRESERVE all existing text. v0.0.2's content should remain intact; this is an
  insertion, not a rewrite. (The fox/stream scene and the entire cabin/bedtime
  scene must survive unchanged in substance.)
- Match the established character voices: Big Bear slow/calm, Middle Bear
  practical ("Everything in its place"), Little Bear curious/small.
- The insertion seam must read smoothly — the transition out of the fox scene
  and into the blueberry scene, and out of the blueberry scene into the arrival
  home, should both flow naturally.
- Title may stay "The Little Lost Star" (the bedtime arc is still the heart).

## Acceptance / what the operator will judge (prose — human read, not grep)

- The blueberry scene appears in the INTERIOR (between the fox farewell and the
  cabin arrival), NOT prepended or appended.
- All v0.0.2 text is preserved.
- Voices and tone match.
- The two new seams (in and out of the blueberry scene) read smoothly.
- Published to projects/pgai-three-bears/artifacts/v0.0.3-*.md (v0.0.2 retained).
